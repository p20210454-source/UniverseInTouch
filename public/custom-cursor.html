★ Featured paper On the Convergence of Stochastic Gradient Descent with Adaptive Learning Rates in Non-Convex Optimization
Zhang W., Patel R., Müller S., Okonkwo C.

We establish new convergence guarantees for stochastic gradient descent methods equipped with adaptive learning rates, proving O(1/√T) convergence to first-order stationary points under mild smoothness assumptions. Our analysis unifies several popular algorithms including Adam, RMSProp, and Adagrad within a single theoretical framework, resolving long-standing questions about their asymptotic behavior in the non-convex regime. Experiments on large language model pretraining confirm the theoretical predictions.

Spectral Gaps in Random Matrix Ensembles and Their Connection to the Riemann Hypothesis
Nakamura T., Bloch E., Ferreira A.

We explore deep connections between the eigenvalue statistics of random Hermitian matrices from the GUE and the non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function, extending the Montgomery-Odlyzko law to a new class of L-functions over function fields.

A New Proof of the Poincaré Conjecture via Geometric Flows in Higher Dimensions
Osei K., Lindström P., Pham Q.T.

Building on Perelman's seminal work, we present a simplified proof of the Poincaré conjecture using a novel monotonicity formula for the Ricci flow with surgery, extending the argument to characterize simply-connected compact 4-manifolds.
